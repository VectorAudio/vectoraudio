Next-Generation Audio Format

Vector Audio™

A model-based audio standard for dynamic rendering, adaptive playback, and expressive performance encoding — designed to move beyond static waveform formats.

What is Vector Audio?

Vector Audio™ is a proposed digital audio framework that represents music as performance data, instrument and vocal models, and scene descriptions rather than fixed PCM samples. At playback time, a render engine synthesizes the final audio in real time, allowing content to improve as models, devices, and listening environments evolve.

Instead of a single frozen “recording,” Vector Audio describes how a performance is played and how it should sound, making it possible to adapt to new devices, rooms, and creative contexts without re-recording or remastering.

Goals

  • Define a model-based audio format that is resolution-independent and future-proof.
  • Preserve performance expressivity at a deeper level than MIDI or traditional project files.
  • Support adaptive playback based on device, room, and listener preferences.
  • Enable upgradeable masters as models and render engines improve over time.
  • Provide a structured path for human–AI collaboration in music creation and playback.
